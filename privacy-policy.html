PickleCall Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 4, 2026

PickleCall is a local-first camera application designed for pickleball line calling. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data PickleCall accesses and how it is handled.

Camera Access

PickleCall accesses your device camera to perform real-time ball detection using on-device machine learning. Camera frames are processed entirely on your device and are never saved, uploaded, or transmitted to any server.

Local Network

PickleCall uses your local Wi-Fi network to communicate between camera nodes and the referee host device during multi-camera sessions. This communication stays on your local network. No internet connection is required or used for core functionality.

Audio

PickleCall plays audio alerts through your device speaker to signal line calls. Audio playback may override the device silent mode as permitted by the operating system.

Data Storage

All call history, session logs, and video clips are stored locally on your device. You may export or delete this data at any time through the app settings. No data is synced to external servers.

Data Collection

PickleCall does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, or any form of user tracking.

Third-Party Services

PickleCall does not use any third-party analytics, crash reporting, advertising, or data processing services.

Children's Privacy

PickleCall does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. The app is safe for use by all ages.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated revision date.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please contact us at picklecall@hotmail.com.
